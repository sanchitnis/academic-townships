# Polycentric Charter for Academic Townships (v0.1)

## Preamble
This charter defines the intent and guardrails for building master-planned academic townships integrated with industrial corridors, while preserving institutional autonomy and public-interest outcomes.

## Pillars of intent
1. **Autonomy + Shared Commons**
   - Each institution retains academic and governance autonomy.
   - Shared labs, incubators, and R&D clusters are managed as commons.
2. **Industry-Linked Innovation**
   - Academic research and industrial deployment are connected through transparent collaboration protocols.
3. **Inclusion & Regional Equity**
   - District design must improve access across socioeconomic backgrounds and regions.
4. **Employability & Entrepreneurship**
   - Curricula, labs, and incubation pathways should enable workforce readiness and startup creation.
5. **Sustainable Urban-Education Integration**
   - Township planning must align with ecological and mobility constraints.

## Governance baseline
- Human committee remains final signoff authority during Phase 1.
- External experts provide adversarial and domain-grounded review.
- AI agents assist through structured critique, synthesis, and draft generation.

## Open baseline tensions
- Shared-resource governance vs full autonomy.
- National corridor priorities vs local context.
- Rapid employability outcomes vs long-horizon research goals.
